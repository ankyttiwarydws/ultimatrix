Narendra Modi Stadium, India

Location: Ahmedabad, India
Capacity: 132,000
Year Opened: 1982 (renovated 2020)

The Narendra Modi Stadium is the largest cricket stadium in the world by capacity. This modern marvel boasts state-of-the-art facilities, four dressing rooms, 11 center pitches, and two practice grounds. Since its major renovation, it has hosted high-profile matches, including the 2023 ICC Cricket World Cup Final. The sheer scale and cutting-edge design make it a bucket-list destination for every cricket enthusiast.

Melbourne Cricket Ground (MCG), Australia

Location: Melbourne, Australia
Capacity: 100,024
Year Opened: 1853

The iconic Melbourne Cricket Ground, affectionately called "The G," is Australia's largest and most historic cricket ground. It has witnessed countless sporting milestones, including multiple Cricket World Cup finals, the Boxing Day Test, and even the 1956 Summer Olympics. The MCG‚Äôs electric atmosphere, combined with its rich history and stunning city views, provides a truly unforgettable experience for visitors.

Eden Gardens, India

Location: Kolkata, India
Capacity: 66,000
Year Opened: 1864

Known as the "Mecca of Indian Cricket," Eden Gardens is steeped in tradition and passion. This iconic stadium is famous for its raucous crowds and has been the stage for some of cricket‚Äôs most memorable moments, including the 1987 ICC Cricket World Cup Final and the thrilling 2016 ICC World T20 Final. The ground is not just a venue but an emotion for millions of cricket fans.

Kensington Oval, Barbados

Location: Bridgetown, Barbados
Capacity: 30,500
Year Opened: 1871

The Kensington Oval is the crown jewel of Caribbean cricket. After a massive renovation for the 2007 ICC Cricket World Cup, it now features modern amenities while retaining its old-world charm. With its picturesque setting and festive crowds, watching a match at Kensington Oval is a truly Caribbean experience.

Imperial Wanderers Stadium, South Africa

Location: Johannesburg, South Africa
Capacity: 34,000
Year Opened: 1956
