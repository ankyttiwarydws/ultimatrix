Classroom Hustle to Digital Brand: How HairbyHearboy Built a Hair Business Through Social Media

Kate Roland The expansion of social media has reshaped how young Nigerians build careers, turning platforms like TikTok and Instagram into powerful tools for entrepreneurship, branding, and customer acquisition. In this evolving digital economy, creators are increasingly converting personal skills into scalable businesses with little more than a smartphone and consistency. One example of this shift is Chinedu Akanigwo, popularly known online as HairbyHearboy , a young Nigerian entrepreneur who has built a growing hair business and personal brand through digital content creation. Early Beginnings in Hair Entrepreneurship Chinedu’s journey into the hair industry began in 2016 while he was still in high school. What started as a personal interest gradually developed into a side hustle, driven by a desire to build something of his own. Working with limited resources, he began honing his craft early, laying the foundation for what would later become a social media-driven business. Born on November 4, 2001, and raised in Lagos, Nigeria, Chinedu grew up in a family of four children. His parents, Okechukwu Raphael Akanigwo and Celestina Akanigwo, reportedly supported his creative ambitions and encouraged his pursuit of entrepreneurship from an early stage. His education took place at Prime Foundation Primary School and later Busy Brain Secondary School. Turning Point: TikTok and Digital Visibility A major shift in Chinedu’s growth came in 2022, when he began creating content on TikTok. Using the platform to showcase hair styling techniques, tutorials, and professional tips, he gradually built an online audience. Within a few years, he grew his following to over 17,000 users, leveraging consistent posting and practical, skill-based content. His videos often focus on hair expertise and styling education, helping to position him as both a service provider and a digital educator within his niche. As his visibility increased, his work began attracting attention beyond his immediate audience, including interactions with public figures and online personalities such as Peller, Jarvis, and Umeneanya, further amplifying his reach. Building a Personal Brand in the Creator Economy Chinedu’s growth reflects a broader trend in Nigeria’s creator economy, where social media is no longer just a communication tool but a commercial ecosystem. By combining technical skill with digital storytelling, creators like him are able to convert attention into business opportunities and customer trust. Despite his growing recognition, he is described as maintaining a grounded approach to his journey, attributing his progress to consistency, dedication, and timing rather than hype alone. His content continues to focus on practical value, particularly in hair care and styling techniques, which remains central to his brand identity. A Glimpse Into the Future of Digital Entrepreneurship As platforms like TikTok continue to shape consumer behavior and business discovery, young entrepreneurs are increasingly finding alternative pathways to success outside traditional employment structures. Chinedu Akanigwo’s trajectory highlights how niche skills, when paired with consistent content creation and audience engagement, can evolve into sustainable personal brands. In a digital landscape that rewards visibility and authenticity, his story reflects a growing class of self-made entrepreneurs redefining how business is built in Nigeria’s online economy.
