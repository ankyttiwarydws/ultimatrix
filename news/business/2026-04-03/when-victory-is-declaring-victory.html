When victory is declaring victory

Victory is what you say victory is. Leaders, even when they haven't won the war (or 'special military operation') seek to have the commanding hand on narratives, redefining outcomes until the word itself collapses under contradictions. What, indeed, is victory? It is the ability to insist upon it, loudly and frequently enough, so that any doubts are, if not cleared, ridden roughshod over. Call it the 'fluidity of outcome'.
