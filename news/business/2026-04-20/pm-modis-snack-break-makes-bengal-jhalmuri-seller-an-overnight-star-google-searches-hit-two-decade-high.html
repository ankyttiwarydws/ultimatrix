PM Modi’s ‘snack break’ makes Bengal jhalmuri seller an ‘overnight star’, Google searches hit two-decade high

A brief stop for a plate of jhalmuri has turned into one of the most talked-about political moments in West Bengal’s election season. Prime Minister Narendra Modi paused during his campaign tour in Jhargram on April 19 to enjoy the iconic Bengali street snack, creating a viral social media moment and making a local vendor an overnight sensation. Related Story: PM Modi takes a ‘Jhalmuri Break’ in Jhargram during Bengal campaign, video wins hearts; watch A Simple Snack, A Massive Viral Moment Videos and photos of the Prime Minister standing beside a modest roadside stall, chatting with the vendor and eating jhalmuri, flooded social media within hours. According to reports, the clip crossed 100 million views across platforms within a day, while Instagram and Facebook engagement surged dramatically. During the interaction, PM Modi asked the vendor to prepare the snack “properly” and insisted on paying for it despite the seller’s reluctance. He even joked when asked if he eats onions, saying he eats onions but not “people’s brains,” a line that quickly became widely shared online. Vendor Deepak Kumar Becomes Overnight 'Star' The man behind the viral stall is Deepak Kumar, a migrant from Bihar who studied till Class 9 before leaving school to support his family. Deepak shared he had no idea the Prime Minister would stop at his shop. “After eating, he said that he liked it very much. He asked my name and also asked what level I had studied,” Deepak recalled, adding that Modi spent nearly 10 minutes at the stall and interacted warmly with children nearby. Ever since PM Modi's visit, curious locals and visitors have been flocking to the Jhalmuri stall, with many sharing videos of their visit across social media platforms.
