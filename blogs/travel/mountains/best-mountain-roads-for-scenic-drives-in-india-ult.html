Leh-Manali Highway (Himachal Pradesh to Ladakh)

Spanning over 475 kilometers, this route connects Manali in Himachal Pradesh with the high-altitude region of Ladakh. Gangtok to Nathu-La Pass (Sikkim)

Bengaluru to Munnar (Karnataka to Kerala)

For a scenic drive that combines wildlife, rolling hills, and endless tea plantations, the route from Bengaluru to Munnar is unbeatable. The 480 km journey takes you through Bandipur National Park, the misty Nilgiris, and finally to the emerald-green slopes of Munnar in Kerala. Along the way, you can spot elephants, visit tea factories, and relax by the Mattupetty Dam. The best time to visit is from October to May, avoiding the monsoons.[1]

Manali to Srinagar (Himachal Pradesh to Kashmir)

Extending the journey from Leh, the Manali-Srinagar route is a dream for those who want to witness the changing face of the Himalayas. The route traverses high-altitude deserts before descending into the lush valleys of Kashmir. The cultural shift is palpable, from Buddhist monasteries in Ladakh to the Islamic heritage of Srinagar. The highlight is gliding across Dal Lake or wandering through ancient villages where time seems to stand still.[3]

Mumbai to Goa via NH 66 (Western Ghats)

While not strictly a mountain road, the Mumbai to Goa drive on NH 66 offers a mesmerizing journey along the Western Ghats. The route is dotted with coconut groves, cascading waterfalls, and panoramic sea views, weaving through quaint Konkan villages before reaching the sun-drenched beaches of Goa. This drive offers a perfect blend of mountain and coastal beauty.[4]

Travel Tips for Mountain Roads in India

  • Plan your trip in advance: Many mountain routes close during winter due to snow or landslides.
  • Check permit requirements: Some roads, especially in border areas like Nathu-La, require special permits.
  • Be prepared for altitude: Carry medicines and acclimatize gradually to avoid altitude sickness.
  • Drive cautiously: Mountain roads can be narrow and unpredictable. Respect local speed limits and be alert for wildlife or landslides.
  • Pack essentials: Carry extra water, snacks, and warm clothing, as services may be limited in remote areas.
