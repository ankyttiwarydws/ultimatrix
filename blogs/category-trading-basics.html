Trading Basics — Everything You Wish Someone Had Explained From the Start

The foundation that makes everything else make sense.

Here is something nobody tells you when you first open a trading account: the biggest reason most beginners lose money early on has nothing to do with picking the wrong stocks. It is that nobody explained the basics properly before they started.

What is the difference between buying a stock and trading a stock? What does "delivery" actually mean, and why does it matter? What is intraday trading, and why is it significantly harder than it looks on YouTube? What are futures and options, and why do experienced traders treat them differently from equity? These are not complicated questions — but if nobody has answered them clearly for you, every step forward feels shakier than it should.

This section exists to fix that.

Trading Basics covers the foundational ideas that every trader needs — regardless of whether you have been in the market for a week or a year and feel like you missed something. Nothing here assumes you already know the terminology. Every concept is explained from scratch, with real examples and real numbers, the way a knowledgeable friend would explain it if you asked them over chai.

If you are completely new, start at the first article and work through in order. If you have been trading for a while but still feel like some of the foundations are fuzzy, pick the article whose title matches the thing that has been nagging at you. Either way, you are in the right place.
