GTA 5 Flight School: Tips for Passing All Lessons

Flight School in Grand Theft Auto V is a crucial activity for players wanting to master the skies of Los Santos. Whether you’re after 100% completion, trying to max out your flying skill, or just want to dominate GTA Online’s aerial missions, Flight School provides a structured way to sharpen your aviation abilities. This guide breaks down essential tips for passing all Flight School lessons, helping you earn those coveted gold medals and making your time in the cockpit much smoother.

Why Complete Flight School?

Flight School isn’t just for perfectionists. Completing lessons not only improves your pilot stats in both story mode and GTA Online, but you’ll also unlock rewards, cash, and reputation points (RP) online. The better your medal, the bigger your payout, making it a great way to kickstart your GTA fortune, especially if you’re new to flying or want an edge in missions involving aircraft.

How to Access Flight School

In GTA 5’s story mode, you can access Flight School at Los Santos International Airport. In GTA Online, the San Andreas Flight School is available after you reach Rank 6. Look for the icon at the airport and make sure it’s not hidden on your map. Simply walk into the school to start the lessons. Each lesson introduces a new skill or challenge, progressing from takeoffs and landings to advanced aerial stunts and parachuting.
Tip: If you’re ever struggling with a lesson, you can retry as many times as you like without penalty—use this to your advantage to practice!

General Flight School Tips

  • Use a controller: Aircraft are easiest to control with analog sticks, especially for delicate maneuvers.
  • Stay calm and steady: Overcorrecting can cause crashes. Small, gentle adjustments are key.
  • Know your aircraft: Planes handle differently from helicopters. Learn the basics for each before attempting the harder lessons.
  • Watch for checkpoints: Always keep an eye on the next objective marker and line up early.
  • Practice makes perfect: Don’t get discouraged by failures; the lessons are designed to be challenging.

Lesson-by-Lesson Tips

1. Training Takeoff

This is a straightforward lesson. Hold the throttle (RT/R2) to accelerate, then gently pull back on the left stick to lift off. Once airborne, stabilize the plane and fly through the checkpoints. Retract your landing gear to go faster[1][2].

2. Runway Landing

Approach the landing site smoothly. Extend your landing gear (L3) before touching down. Reduce speed, aim for a gentle touchdown, and use brakes (LT/L2) to slow down. Steer carefully to complete the landing in the marked area[1][3].

3. Inverted Flight

Climb to a safe altitude before attempting stunts. To perform a barrel roll, push the left stick left or right in a full circle. For inverted flight, rotate the plane upside down and hold steady. Maintain altitude to avoid crashing into buildings[1][3].

4. Knife Flight

Knife Flight requires flying the plane sideways (wings vertical). Use the left stick to tilt the plane and keep it steady through checkpoints. Make gentle corrections to avoid over-rolling[1][3].

5. Flat Hatting

This lesson tests your low-altitude flying. Stay just above ground or water level, and avoid sudden climbs or dips. Watch out for obstacles and use the rudders to fine-tune your path[3].

6. Touch Down

Practice landing on a specific spot. Approach slowly, keep landing gear down, and aim for the center of the target. Brake gently to stop within the marked area[1].

7. Loop the Loop

Pull back on the stick to perform a loop. Maintain steady speed and altitude to avoid stalling. Complete the loop smoothly, then line up for the next checkpoint[1][2].

8. Helicopter Course

Helicopters require different controls. Use both sticks: one for pitch/roll, one for yaw. Fly steadily through the gates. Small stick movements help prevent erratic flying[3].

9. Helicopter Speed Run

Speed is key, but be careful of obstacles like bridges and metal structures. Keep a low altitude where required, and use the rudders for tight turns without losing control[3].

10. Skydiving

Jump out, wait a moment, and deploy your parachute at a safe altitude. Use L1+R1 (or LB+RB) to slow your descent and steer with the left stick. Aim for the center of the landing zone for max points[3].

11. Drop Zone

This lesson is tougher—land a parachute on a moving target. Circle above your target to line up your approach, descend slowly with L1+R1, and time your landing for when the object is beneath you[3].

12. Earn Your Wings

The final test combines all previous skills. Expect knife flights, inverted maneuvers, and obstacle dodging. The most difficult parts are weaving through downtown buildings and flying inverted over Vespucci Beach. Take your time, stay high when possible, and react quickly to avoid obstacles[3].

GTA Online Flight School Differences

In GTA Online, Flight School features 10 solo lessons, with each completed for a cash and RP reward. The structure is similar, but content may differ slightly from story mode. Earning gold medals on your first attempt yields the biggest bonuses, so aim for precision and speed. Lessons include formation flying, engine failures, and more advanced helicopter skills[4][5].

Extra Tips for Gold Medals

  • Restart if needed: If your run is messy, restart immediately to save time and frustration.
  • Master the controls: Spend some free-roam time flying and parachuting to get comfortable outside of Flight School.
  • Watch tutorials: YouTube guides can be invaluable for visual learners—see the video above for step-by-step walkthroughs.
  • Use in-game hints: Pay attention to the instructor’s tips and on-screen prompts.
