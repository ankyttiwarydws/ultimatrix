GTA 5 Controls for PC

On PC, GTA 5 utilizes a combination of keyboard and mouse inputs, offering precise aiming and quick access to functions. Here is a breakdown of the most essential controls:

  • Movement: W (forward), S (back), A (left), D (right)
  • Jump: SPACEBAR
  • Sprint: Left SHIFT
  • Enter Vehicle: F
  • Stealth Mode: Left CTRL
  • Look Behind (on foot): C
  • Aim Weapon/Lock-On: Right Mouse Button (RMB)
  • Fire Weapon: Left Mouse Button (LMB)
  • Reload: R
  • Take Cover: Q
  • Weapon Wheel: TAB
  • Switch Weapons: Mouse Wheel
  • Character Selector: Left ALT
  • Pause: P
  • Special Ability: Caps Lock

PC players can seamlessly switch between mouse/keyboard and a connected controller, even during gameplay, allowing for flexible control preferences.[1][3]

GTA 5 Controls for Xbox

On Xbox One and Xbox Series X|S, GTA 5 controls are mapped to the gamepad, designed for intuitive access and quick reactions:

  • Movement: Left Stick
  • Camera: Right Stick
  • Sprint: A Button (hold)
  • Jump: X Button
  • Take Cover: RB
  • Enter Vehicle: Y Button
  • Fire Weapon: RT
  • Aim: LT
  • Reload: X Button
  • Weapon Wheel: LB (hold)
  • Switch Characters: D-Pad (Down)
  • Pause: Start Button
  • Special Ability: L3 + R3 (click sticks together)

Players can remap controls to suit their preferences, and with the new consoles, up to 30 players can join GTA Online sessions.[2]

GTA 5 Controls for PlayStation

The control scheme for PlayStation 4 and PlayStation 5 is similar to Xbox, with a few platform-specific features:

  • Movement: Left Stick
  • Camera: Right Stick
  • Sprint: X Button (hold)
  • Jump: Square Button
  • Take Cover: R1
  • Enter Vehicle: Triangle Button
  • Fire Weapon: R2
  • Aim: L2
  • Reload: Square Button
  • Weapon Wheel: L1 (hold)
  • Switch Characters: D-Pad (Down)
  • Pause: Options Button
  • Special Ability: L3 + R3 (click sticks together)

First-Person Mode and Control Customization

All platforms support first-person mode, which brings new animations and control options. Players can toggle between first- and third-person perspectives on foot, in vehicles, and during cover. In first-person, controls are more in line with traditional FPS games, and options like head-bobbing and field of view are customizable for greater comfort.[2]

GTA 5 offers extensive control remapping for all platforms, letting players adjust key bindings and button layouts to match their gaming style. This is particularly helpful for accessibility and for players transitioning from other game genres.
