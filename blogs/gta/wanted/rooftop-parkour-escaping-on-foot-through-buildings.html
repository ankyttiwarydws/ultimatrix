What is Rooftop Parkour?

Techniques for Moving Across Rooftops

While parkour on the ground relies on vaults and wall runs, rooftop parkour demands a unique toolkit:

  • Precision Jumps: Athletes often need to leap from one rooftop to another. Measuring the gap, gauging the landing surface, and executing a controlled jump are crucial for safety and success.
    Tip: Always practice jumps at ground level before attempting them at height, and never attempt a gap unless you are absolutely sure of your abilities.1
  • Cat Leaps and Climb-Ups: Sometimes a rooftop is just out of reach. A cat leap allows you to grab onto the edge and pull yourself up with a climb-up technique.
  • Parkour Rolls: Landing from a jump can put a lot of stress on your body. The parkour roll helps disperse impact, protecting joints and minimizing risk.2
  • Wall Runs and Descents: Moving down from a rooftop can be just as challenging as getting up. Wall runs, tic-tacs (using a wall as a springboard), and controlled drops are essential skills.2

Safety First: Minimizing Risks

Rooftop parkour is inherently risky, but experienced practitioners take extensive precautions:

  • Start Small: Train on the ground and in controlled environments before attempting rooftop moves.
  • Scout the Area: Before making any jump or movement, carefully inspect surfaces for stability, slipperiness, or hidden hazards.1
  • Know Your Limits: Even advanced athletes sometimes walk away from a jump if conditions aren‚Äôt right. Listen to your body and never let ego override caution.1
  • Train With Others: Having a spotter or fellow practitioner can provide guidance and assistance in case of an emergency.

Many experienced athletes stress that rooftop parkour is not for beginners and should only be attempted after years of careful, progressive training.1
