Numbers Stations: The Broadcasts No One Claims

Numbers stations are among the most enduring mysteries on the airwaves. Anyone with a shortwave radio can tune in and hear voices reading out strings of numbers, sometimes layered over haunting music or static. To most listeners, these broadcasts sound like nonsense, but they are believed to be highly secure means of communication between governments and agents. Only someone with the right cipher can decode the message being sent [5].

In some cases, enthusiasts have managed to track down the physical locations of these broadcasts. The Yosemite Sam shortwave incident is a famous example, where a mysterious transmission featuring the Warner Brothers cartoon character Yosemite Sam was traced to a facility on the Laguna Indian reservation in New Mexico. The story, which unfolded between 2004 and 2005, remains a favorite among shortwave radio fans [3].
