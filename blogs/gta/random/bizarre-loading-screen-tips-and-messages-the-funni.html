Bizarre Loading Screen Tips and Messages: The Funniest Waiting Moments in Gaming and Apps

We’ve all been there: staring at a spinning icon or progress bar, waiting for a game or app to load. But some developers have turned this necessary pause into an opportunity for delight, dropping in strange, witty, or downright bizarre messages and tips that have become legendary among users. From sarcastic advice to surreal jokes, these loading screens transform boring wait times into memorable moments. Let’s explore the weirdest and most wonderful loading screen tips ever, and why they matter more than you might think.

Why Do Loading Screens Have Tips and Messages?

Loading screens exist to fill the gap while games or applications fetch assets, process data, or connect online. But instead of letting users stew in impatience, many designers use these screens to:

  • Entertain: Light-hearted jokes or pop culture references keep spirits high while you wait.
  • Inform: Real gameplay tips, helpful hints, or reminders about controls and settings.
  • Build Brand Personality: Quirky or offbeat messages reinforce the app’s unique voice and style.
  • Reduce Perceived Wait Times: Engaged users are less likely to notice or be frustrated by delays.

Some companies take this to the next level, using humor and creativity to make their loading screens unforgettable [3].

Legendary Examples of Bizarre Loading Screen Tips

The world of gaming and tech is packed with oddball loading advice and messages. Here are some classics:

Minecraft: “Do People Actually Read These?”

Minecraft is legendary for its tongue-in-cheek loading messages. One of the most meta: “Do people actually read these?” It’s a playful nod to the sea of tips players usually ignore, poking fun at itself and the whole idea of loading advice [2].

Insurgency: Snack Advice

Some games sneak in genuinely helpful (if unexpected) life tips. Insurgency once advised: “Eating chips while playing can get your controls greasy. Try pretzels, crackers, or baby carrots.” Suddenly, you’re considering a healthier snack mid-battle [2].

Skyrim: Friendship for Loot

Skyrim is known for both its epic quests and its subtly funny tips. A classic: “Become friends with someone, and they may allow you to take certain items from their home.” Only in the world of Tamriel does friendship mean free stuff [2].

Developer In-Jokes and Meta Humor

Some loading messages are pure absurdity or inside jokes only a programmer could love:

  • “Help, I’m trapped in a loader!”
  • “Downloading more RAM…”
  • “Your computer has a virus, its name is Windows!”
  • “Deleting System32 folder.”
  • “Bored of slow loading spinner? Buy more RAM!”
  • “Go ahead, hold your breath and do an ironman plank till loading complete.”
  • “Please wait while we purge the Decepticons for you. Yes, you can thank us later!”
  • “Chuck Norris once urinated in a semi truck’s gas tank as a joke… that truck is now known as Optimus Prime.”

These lines, collected from apps and websites, show just how far developers will go to make you smile [1].

Apps and Websites Join the Fun

It’s not just games. Social media platforms like Letterboxd display funny movie quotes during loading, while project management tools like Asana offer productivity tips, and sites like Tumblr and Reddit rotate through random, often hilarious loading phrases [3].

Why Bizarre Loading Tips Work

What makes these quirky messages so sticky? It’s about more than just a cheap laugh. Bizarre loading tips:

  • Humanize the experience: They remind us that real people made the software.
  • Boost brand loyalty: Unique humor makes users feel part of an inside joke, deepening their connection with the product.
  • Turn frustration into fun: Even a long wait is easier to handle when you’re amused or surprised.

In UX design, a well-crafted loading screen can set the tone and ensure users leave with a smile—even if just for a few seconds [3].

How to Create Your Own Memorable Loading Messages

If you’re designing a product, don’t underestimate the power of a good loading message. Here are a few tips:

  • Know your audience’s humor—don’t be afraid to be weird, but keep it appropriate.
  • Mix practical tips with playful nonsense for variety.
  • Use pop culture references or puns to add personality.
  • Rotate messages to keep things fresh for repeat users.
