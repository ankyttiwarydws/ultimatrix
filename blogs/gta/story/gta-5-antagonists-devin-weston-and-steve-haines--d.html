Who Is Devin Weston?

Devin Weston is the archetype of the modern corporate villain: a billionaire industrialist with immense influence, little regard for ethics, and a penchant for bending the law to his will. As a major shareholder in several companies, including Merryweather Security, Weston sees people as tools and the law as an obstacle to be maneuvered around rather than followed. His ego, ambition, and lack of empathy make him a formidable adversary for the protagonists.

Weston's approach to business and life is ruthlessly pragmatic. He frequently manipulates the Franklin, Michael, and Trevor trio, sending them on dangerous missions while withholding payment and ultimately betraying them. His lack of loyalty extends to everyone around him, including his supposed "friends" in law enforcement and government. This makes him a character both feared and despised, even by his allies.

In the climactic moments of GTA 5, Weston's arrogance and disregard for the people he's crossed finally catch up with him, showcasing a classic example of hubris meeting its downfall.

Who Is Steve Haines?

Steve Haines represents institutional corruption at its highest level. As a Special Agent in the FIB (the game's stand-in for the FBI), Haines is both highly decorated and deeply corrupt. He uses his position to manipulate not just criminals, but also fellow agents and government resources for personal gain. Haines is responsible for forcing Michael, Franklin, and Trevor into working for him, leveraging their criminal pasts as blackmail.

Born in 1975 and joining the FIB in 1993, Haines quickly climbed the ranks, eventually commanding his own special unit. Despite his achievements, he is notorious within the agency for putting his interests first, and by 2013, he is known to be on the payroll of wealthy individuals like Devin Weston. Haines‚Äôs dual roles as a law enforcement officer and TV show host further amplify his ego, as he craves public attention and validation as much as power.

His downfall is marked by his inability to maintain control and his tendency to make enemies on all sides. In the end, his own arrogance and reliance on corrupt alliances contribute to his undoing.
